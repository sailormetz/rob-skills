# Shift-Story Carousel — Structure & Format

---

## Story Arc

Every shift-story follows this shape. Slide count is flexible — use as many as the story needs, no padding.

1. **Hook** — Drop the reader into the call. One line that creates immediate tension or curiosity. No drug name required.
2. **Setup** — Who's the patient? What's the situation? What did you walk into?
3. **Escalation** — Things get complicated. The thing that made this call memorable.
4. **Turn / Decision** — What did you do? Why? The moment of action.
5. **Outcome** — What happened. Keep it honest.
6. **Lesson** — One sentence. What does this call teach? Not a lecture — a takeaway a medic can carry.
7. **CTA** — Engagement question + resource hint.

---

## Hook Rules

One line. Drop the reader into the moment. No preamble.

- Present tense or past tense — whichever feels more immediate.
- Creates tension, curiosity, or recognition ("I've been there").
- Don't introduce yourself. Don't set the scene. Just start.

**Examples:**
```
The kid was three years old and wasn't breathing.
We got toned out at 2am and I knew something was wrong before we arrived.
She told me she was fine. Her BP said otherwise.
```

---

## Slide Limits

- **200 characters max per slide** (markup stripped). Sparse is the point — each slide should feel like one moment, not a paragraph.
- No minimum slide count. Cut anything that doesn't move the story.

---

## Slide Format

No section labels. No teasers. Just text — one moment per slide.

```
[1]  Hook line.

[2]  Setup. One or two sentences.

[3]  What happened next.

[N]  The lesson. One sentence.

[N]  CTA line 1
     CTA line 2
```
