# Shift-Story Carousel — Voice & Tone

---

## Voice

Write like you're telling a friend about a call on the way back to the station. Casual, first-person, present-tense energy. Not a script, not a report — a story.

- Short, punchy sentences. Each one moves the story forward.
- Emotion is allowed. Tension, relief, uncertainty, humor — whatever fits the call.
- No clinical lecturer voice. Don't define terms or explain pharmacology mid-story.
- No color tags, no section labels, no teasers. Let the story carry it.
- One emoji max per carousel (hook only, if it fits naturally).

---

## CTA Format

Two lines, plain text. Personal and conversational — the call was relatable, the CTA should be too.

**Line 1 — Engagement (rotate one per carousel):**
```
Have you had a call like this? Tell me in the comments.
What's a call that stuck with you? Drop it in the comments.
What would you have done differently? Let me know in the comments.
```

**Line 2 — Resource hint (always the same):**
```
I've broken down every drug in EMS just like this. Link in bio.
```

No clinical quiz format. Invite people to share their own stories.
