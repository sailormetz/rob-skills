# Shift-Story Carousel — Voice & Tone

---

## Voice

Write like you're telling a friend about a call on the drive back to the station. Not writing — *talking*. The difference matters.

- **Let thoughts breathe.** A slide can have line breaks between beats without being fragmented. Space creates rhythm.
- **Don't over-fragment.** Some moments belong together on one slide. "I took him and started compressions on the kitchen table. My partner bagged." — that's one slide, not two.
- **Soften transitions naturally.** "And we got ROSC in about four minutes." The "And" is intentional — it's how people actually talk.
- **Let the reflection do real work.** Don't rush to the lesson. The narrator sitting with the moment IS part of the story.
- **Concrete lessons, not vague sentiment.** "It's best to assume that screen with call notes is just wrong" beats "always be prepared." Say the actual thing.
- No color tags. No section labels. No clinical lecturer voice.
- One emoji max per carousel, hook only, only if it fits naturally.

---

## CTA Format

Two lines. Simple and human — match the tone of the story.

**Line 1 — Engagement (rotate one per carousel):**
```
If you've been there, you know.
Have you had a call like this? Tell me in the comments.
What's a call that stuck with you? Drop it in the comments.
What would you have done differently? Let me know in the comments.
```

**Line 2 — Channel hint:**
```
Follow for more medic wisdom.
```

Keep it simple. No clinical quiz format. No sales language.
